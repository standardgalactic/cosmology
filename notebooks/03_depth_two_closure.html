Why dclosure(Φc) = 2: eigenvalue spectrum of the projected accessibility Hessian

The Core Result

The ATV spectrum of eigenvalues of the projected Hessian at SM is:

λ_A^n = 2n/3 > 0 ∀n≥1
λ_B^1 = −1 (gauge)
λ_B^2 = −1/3 (physical)
λ_B^n = (2n−5)/3 > 0 ∀n≥3

Only two negative eigenvalues exist. After gauge-fixing removes λ_B^1, a single stable direction remains at depth 2. All deeper levels are positive.

This means: knowing a trajectory is in U₂(Φ_c) guarantees it is in Uₙ(Φ_c) for all n≥2.

Information Flow

At each depth level, two new degrees of freedom (z_{2n}, w_{2n-2}) enter the hierarchy. The stability of each level is determined by the sign of λ_B^n.

Depth 1: one positive + one negative (gauge) — indeterminate

Depth 2: adds λ_B^2 = −1/3 — physical stable direction identified

Depth 3+: both eigenvalues positive — no new stable directions

Conclusion: closure is achieved exactly at depth 2.

Comparison

If the spectrum had negative λ_B^n at every order (like KAM tori in Hamiltonian systems), closure depth would be ∞. The Friedmann instability is exceptional: it closes at depth 2.
