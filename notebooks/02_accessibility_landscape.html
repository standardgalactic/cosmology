Geodesics under the accessibility metric gaccij = gij + κ ∂iΦ ∂jΦ

Physics

The accessibility metric deforms geodesics by penalising motion through regions of high |∇Φ|. Particles seek paths of least accessibility cost.

ds²acc = gijdxidxj + κ(∂kΦ dxk)²

When κ=0, geodesics are straight lines (Euclidean). As κ increases, paths bend to avoid high-gradient ridges.
